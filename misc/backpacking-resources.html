Recommended Travel Blogs

Beyond the data, the best travel tips often come from those who have lived the experience. We’ve curated a list of leading travel bloggers who specialize in budget exploration and backpacking. These sites are excellent resources for stories, photography, and niche advice to help inspire your next destination.

  • Where Goes Rose

    Where Goes Rose is a well-respected travel blog run by Rose Munday, a professional solo traveler and photographer known for her extensive, boots-on-the-ground guides. The site is a standout resource for backpackers and budget travelers, particularly those interested in deep-dive culinary guides, street art tours, and practical solo-female travel advice. Rose’s content is distinguished by its meticulous detail—often including specific walking routes, local prices, and "hidden gem" locations that go beyond standard tourist checklists. Her expertise is especially strong in regions like Southeast Asia, Mexico, and Europe, making her site an essential stop for travelers looking for a blend of cultural immersion and highly actionable logistics.

  • Gabriela Here And There

    Gabriela Here and There is an insightful travel blog that focuses on cultural experiences and practical advice for independent explorers. Gabriela provides a wealth of information on navigating various destinations with a focus on slow travel and authentic local interactions. Her guides are particularly useful for those seeking a balance between popular landmarks and off-the-beaten-path discoveries, often highlighting sustainable travel practices and budget-friendly tips. With a clear, engaging writing style and a focus on the logistical nuances of solo travel, the site serves as a reliable companion for anyone looking to plan a meaningful and well-organized journey.

  • Indie Traveller

    Indie Traveller, run by seasoned globetrotter Marek Bron, is one of the internet’s premier resources for independent, low-budget, and long-term travel. Stripping away the generic tourist fluff, the blog focuses heavily on the raw logistics of backpacking, providing realistic budgeting guides, packing itineraries, and destination profiles that favor adventure over luxury. Marek excels at breaking down the mental and financial barriers of hit-the-road travel, offering highly practical advice on everything from finding affordable accommodation to navigating local transport networks. It is a fantastic tool for both first-time backpackers needing a comprehensive "how-to" blueprint and veteran travelers looking for raw, unfiltered destination insights.

  • Nomadic Matt

    Nomadic Matt, founded by New York Times bestselling author Matt Kepnes, is widely considered the ultimate blueprint for budget travel and backpacking. The site is built on the philosophy that you don’t need to be rich to travel, offering exhaustive breakdowns on how to explore the world using smart money-saving tactics, hostel hacks, and reward points. Matt and his team provide highly detailed, regularly updated destination guides, gear reviews, and safety tips that cater specifically to independent and long-term backpackers. For anyone looking to stretch their funds further without missing out on authentic local experiences, this site is the definitive industry gold standard.

  • The Broke Backpacker

    The Broke Backpacker, founded by Will Hatton, is a high-energy, gritty resource dedicated to the art of extreme budget travel and adventure backpacking. The site operates on the principle that a tight budget shouldn't limit your horizons, specializing in raw, unfiltered guides to off-the-beaten-path destinations, long-term hitchhiking, and minimalist camping. Alongside comprehensive country guides and hostel recommendations, the platform is highly regarded for its deep-dive, honest reviews of backpacking gear, tents, and digital nomad electronics. It is an ideal community for adventurous, independent travellers who want to stretch their cash to the absolute limit while exploring the more rugged and wild corners of the globe.

  • The Budget Traveller

    Budget Traveller, created by award-winning blogger and author Kash Bhattacharya, completely redefines what it means to explore the world on a shoestring. Rather than focusing strictly on the absolute cheapest options, the blog champions the concept of "flashpacking" and style-led budget travel—proving that a lower budget doesn't mean you have to sacrifice comfort, design, or soul. Kash, named a National Geographic Traveller Blogger of the Year, is a leading voice in the hostel industry and the author of The Grand Hostels: Luxury Hostels of the World. His site features deeply curated guides to high-end design hostels, affordable boutique stays, and cheap eats that don't skimp on quality. It is an exceptional resource for modern, value-driven backpackers who want to save money on accommodation and transport so they can invest it into unforgettable, hyper-local experiences.

  • Bunch Of Backpackers

    Bunch of Backpackers, founded by Dutch travel writer and medical doctor Manouk, is a highly trusted independent travel blog that caters to the modern adventurer. Written from the perspective of a passionate budget explorer who balances a professional career with a love for the road, the site provides a refreshing mix of raw adventure and organised, actionable advice. Manouk specialises in off-the-beaten-track destinations, multi-day treks, and responsible tourism, delivering honest hostel reviews and realistic budget breakdowns that avoid generic tourism fluff. It is an exceptional resource for independent backpackers who want to step out of their comfort zones, maximise their annual leave, and experience immersive cultural journeys without breaking the bank.

  • Goats On The Road

    Goats On The Road, founded by Canadian couple Nick Wharton and Dariece Swift, is a leading resource for long-term budget travellers and digital nomads looking to turn their adventures into a sustainable lifestyle. The site focuses heavily on the practicalities of remote work, providing comprehensive guides on teaching English abroad, house-sitting, and travel blogging alongside their destination tips. Nick and Dariece emphasise location independence, offering real-world financial breakdowns and step-by-step strategies to help backpackers fund their journeys indefinitely. It is an invaluable platform for independent travellers who want to move beyond simple holidaying and transition into a life of continuous, budget-conscious global exploration.

  • The Partying Traveler

    The Partying Traveler, run by seasoned budget explorer Eli Solidum, is a vibrant travel blog that perfectly captures the social and nightlife-driven side of youth travel. Eli focuses on the communal aspect of backpacking, offering highly authentic guides to the world’s best party hostels, festival destinations, and nightlife scenes, while keeping cost and safety firmly in mind. Beyond the social itineraries, the site delivers exceptionally practical, feet-on-the-ground travel logistics, realistic budget breakdowns, and hidden-gem recommendations that ensure you get a well-rounded cultural experience by day. It is an indispensable resource for solo backpackers looking to make lifelong friends, find the best atmosphere on the road, and stretch their travel funds to the absolute maximum.

  • The Pink Backpack

    The Pink Backpack, run by Canadian solo traveller, digital nomad, and author Stephanie Huff, is a wonderfully candid and highly entertaining blog for the modern backpacker. Steering clear of glossy, hyper-curated travel feeds, Stephanie embraces the raw and often chaotic realities of the road, sharing her own hilarious travel mishaps and "misadventures" alongside practical advice. Having explored over 50 countries across six continents—including an extensive 4,000-mile overland backpacking journey through Africa—her site delivers excellent, feet-on-the-ground resources for solo-female travellers and budget conscious explorers. From comprehensive packing guides and hostel etiquette tips to advice on how to travel as a broke university student, it is an inspiring and refreshing platform for anyone looking to challenge themselves and explore the world independently.

  • Kami & The Rest Of The World

    Created by Polish travel writer Kamila Napora, Kami and the Rest of the World (found at mywanderlust.pl) is an exceptional resource for independent travellers looking to explore Central and Eastern Europe, the Balkans, and lesser-known, alternative destinations. Kami specialises in solo-female travel and culture-rich itineraries, delivering highly practical, feet-on-the-ground advice that focuses heavily on train and bus logistics. Her guides are distinctively honest, offering deep dives into street art, alternative culture, 20th-century architecture, and realistic budget tips that skip the generic tourist traps. It is an invaluable site for backpackers who want to dig deeper into a region's history, master the art of independent transport, and uncover fascinating destinations that rarely make the front page of mainstream guidebooks.

  • Waves 'n Backpack

    Founded by Ani and Julian, a travel-loving duo from Cologne, wavesnbackpack is a beautifully visual travel and surf blog designed for independent explorers who love the ocean. The site seamlessly merges the backpacking lifestyle with surf culture, offering highly practical surf guides, destination itineraries, and gear packing lists for coastal hotspots across Europe, Africa, Asia, and Central America. Alongside their detailed city guides and destination breakdowns, Ani and Julian share expert tips for surf beginners and insights into the digital nomad lifestyle, making it an inspiring and incredibly useful resource for backpackers who prefer to travel with a board in tow and sand between their toes.

    • Other Useful Websites

    Beyond the data, the best travel tips often come from those who have lived the experience. We’ve curated a list of leading travel bloggers who specialize in budget exploration and backpacking. These sites are excellent resources for stories, photography, and niche advice to help inspire your next destination.

  • SouthAfrica.net

    The official tourism website, southafrica.net, serves as a comprehensive digital gateway for travellers wishing to explore the diverse landscapes and vibrant cultures of the Rainbow Nation. Managed by South African Tourism, the platform provides an array of essential resources, from detailed destination guides and curated itineraries to practical advice on travel requirements and safety. Whether a visitor is seeking a world-class safari in the Kruger National Park, a sophisticated city break in Cape Town, or an adventurous trek along the Wild Coast, the site expertly showcases the country's unique appeal. Furthermore, it offers dedicated sections for business events and travel trade partners, ensuring that South Africa remains a premier choice for international conferences and global holiday organisers alike.

  • SATSA

    The Southern Africa Tourism Services Association, or SATSA, is a non-profit member-driven organisation that represents the private sector of the inbound tourism industry. By providing a strict code of conduct and a financial bonding scheme, the association ensures that international travellers can book with confidence, knowing that accredited members are vetted for quality, safety, and financial integrity. For those navigating the complexities of Southern African travel, the SATSA website offers a vital membership directory, alongside resources on tourism safety, visa requirements, and industry news. Ultimately, the platform serves as a benchmark for professionalism, helping to foster a reliable and sustainable environment for both local businesses and global visitors exploring the region.

  • SANParks

    South African National Parks, commonly referred to as SANParks, is the official body responsible for managing the country’s vast network of protected areas and diverse ecosystems. Through its comprehensive website, travellers can access essential information for world-renowned destinations such as Kruger National Park, Table Mountain, and the Addo Elephant National Park. The platform provides a centralised system for checking availability and securing bookings for various types of accommodation, ranging from basic campsites to luxury lodges, whilst also offering detailed maps and wildlife sightings updates. By prioritising conservation and sustainable tourism, the organisation ensures that the natural heritage of South Africa remains accessible to those wishing to experience the region’s unique flora and fauna in a responsible manner.

  • Weather - Windy.com

    Windy.com has become an indispensable tool for outdoor enthusiasts and travellers in South Africa, offering exceptionally detailed weather visualisations that surpass standard forecasting apps. By utilising high-resolution models, the platform provides real-time data on wind speed, swell height, and precipitation, which is particularly vital for those navigating the unpredictable coastal conditions of the Western Cape or planning hikes in the Drakensberg. Its interactive interface allows users to track shifting weather patterns with precision, making it a favourite for surfers, sailors, and mountain trekkers who require accurate, localised information to ensure their safety. Whether one is checking for the arrival of the "Cape Doctor" wind or monitoring summer thunderstorms in the Highveld, the site offers a sophisticated and reliable way to plan activities around the natural elements.

  • Currency - XE.com

    For any international traveller navigating the diverse economy of South Africa, XE.com serves as an essential resource for managing finances and understanding the real-time value of the South African Rand. The platform provides a highly reliable currency converter that utilises mid-market rates, offering a clear benchmark for those looking to exchange funds or monitor fluctuations in the foreign exchange market. Beyond simple conversions, the website features historical rate charts and travel expense calculators, which are particularly useful for budget-conscious visitors planning an extended trip across the country. By providing transparent and up-to-date financial data, the site helps travellers avoid unfavourable exchange rates and ensures they can make informed decisions when handling their money abroad.
